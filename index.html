    Introduction

    This is the documentation of JDL version 1.0.0.

    What is Justice Definition Languege?

    The Justice Definition Languege is an open-source model for a human and machine-readable "taxonomy" of justice-related data types, behaviours, and usages. It adopts the Criminal Justice System Exchange Data Standards.

    Developed by Advanced Analytica with input from legal and technical experts in the AI Justice Community, JDL aims to establish a standardised, extensible, and interoperable schema for managing criminal trial data with artififical intelligence. JDL is intended to facilitate the integration of case data with generative AI models via Retrieval Augmented Generative (RAG) systems. By providing a unified framework, JCS ensures that justice-related data is consistent, interoperable, and easily accessible, allowing different systems and platforms to communicate effectively.

    Purpose of JDL

    The purpose of JDL is to develop a common schema that serves as a standard for structuring justice-related data. It aims to: - Standardize Data: Define a unified set of fields and data types used across criminal trial and legal data management systems. - Enhance Interoperability: Enable seamless data exchange and integration between different databases, systems, and applications, supporting AI and generative technologies. - Improve Usability: Create a human-readable and machine-friendly format, making it easier for legal professionals, data scientists, and developers to work with justice-related information efficiently. - Support RAGS: Develop a schema that supports the deployment of Retrieval Augmented Generative Systems into case preparation workflows, enhancing the efficiency and accuracy of legal processes.

    Schema Structure

    JDL specifies field names and data types suitable for various justice-related applications, including criminal case management, legal evidence tracking, and court proceedings. It organizes these fields into levels that indicate the importance and expected usage of each field at different stages of a trial. This structured approach allows for flexible adaptation as cases or needs evolve.

    New to JDL?

    If you’re new to JDL and want to understand its benefits and core concepts, the Getting Started section is a great place to begin.

    My Data Doesn’t Map with JDL

    JDL is designed to be flexible. If your legal data includes additional details that don’t map directly to JDL, you can extend the schema by adding custom fields. Follow the guidelines provided in the Guidelines and Best Practices section to ensure consistency.

    Maturity

    JDL follows Semantic Versioning for its releases. Major updates to JDL are aligned with significant changes and improvements in legal data management technologies and justice processes.

    Feedback on the schema’s structure, missing fields, or areas for improvement is highly encouraged. For contributions, please see the Contribution Guidelines.
